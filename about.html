What is “Let’s Code!” about?

Project “Let’s Code!” is an initiative by Singapore Management University’s (SMU) School of Information Systems (SIS) to introduce pre-university students to basic programming with Ruby. There are 4 scheduled courses, each lasting 3 weeks: June 2016, Dec 2016, June 2017 and Dec 2017.

How would I be taught?

This course is run in blended pedagogy: students are expected to do most of the learning at home via videos with online support, and attend 7 face-to-face classroom sessions at SMU. An examination will be conducted on the final session.

The Face-to-face Sessions for Dec 2016 run (7 sessions over 3 weeks, tentative) are as follows:

  • Course Briefing (compulsory): 1 Dec 2016 (Thu) 2-5pm. Venue: SMU (exact venue to be determined)
  • Tutorials: 5, 8, 12, 15, 19th Dec 2016 (every Mon and Thu), 2-5pm. Venue: SMU
  • Assessment (compulsory): 22 Dec 2016 (Thu) 2-5pm. Venue: SMU
Great! Any eligibility and pre-requisites to attend?

Yes, you would need to sastify the following conditions:

  • You are a current student in JC or Millennia Institute; or a year 4-6 student on the IP/IB program.
  • You should not be taking “Computing” as a subject. This course is designed for pre-u students who have 0 or little programming knowledge, and is not suitable for students who have prior programming knowledge.
  • You must bring your own laptops running Windows or MacOS for the meet-up sessions, and have access to the Internet from home during the course.
